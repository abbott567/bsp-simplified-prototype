RVU Referral

In this iteration we are looking at what happens when you cant verify a marriage and need to refer it to the RVU.

Research notes

It was hypothesised that agents would know that if a marriage was not validated by the RVU they would know to select ‘no’ to whether they have seen evidence to disallow the claim at this point. This was not the case. The decision sent back from the RVU is considered evidence regardless of the outcome of that evidence. When agents were referring a case to the RVU they seemed to be comfortable saying that they haven't seen evidence as they were unable to make a decision based on the information the claimant or the type of information the claimant provided. The valid decision from the RVU seemed to be straightforward for agents to work through as they take this as being their evidence so they could happily say they had seen evidence. In the case where the evidence from the RVU proved that the marriage was not valid agents struggled.

Agents went on to allow this claim as they believed that they could select that they have seen evidence as they have the RVU evidence even though that shows they aren’t married. They knew that they would disallow this claim, but they weren’t able to find the correct route on the system to do this. Some agents selected the wrong options, some selected the wrong options and managed to still disallow for the wrong reason, and some allowed the claim and tried to change their answers but still allowed it for a second time. This did not work well.

It’s extremely rare for the RVU to give an unknown decision, so we didn’t test this scenario as it would have added extra confusion similar to the disallowance route.

One thing that agents raised is that some international claimants won’t have a NINO, they also won’t allocate them one unless they are going to be paid. We need to consider how this would work alongside MI for the future.
