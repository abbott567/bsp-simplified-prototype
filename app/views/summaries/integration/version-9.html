Research notes

1 agent changed the rate via the payment schedule and 16 agents did so via the claim details tab. All agents added a pregnancy to the claim via the children details section and most agents added the child benefit number via the child benefit task although some chose to do this via the children details. These scenarios worked well, agents could quickly understand where and how to change the rate on the schedule and were happy that no other information would be required in order to make the change or to understand what had happened to a claim if viewing from an enquiry point of view. Taking agents to the payment schedule once the change had been confirmed gave them further confidence that the rate had changed as they still check the payments to see that the correct backdating and uprating has taken place.

View payment schedule only - 10/17 agents understood the message shown to them without the need for explanation or prompting, this is the most positive response we’ve had when testing this feature. We stated that the claim was processed using an older version of the system and during the feedback session there were concerns from team members that the word ‘claim’ is used too many times so this can be changed for future testing. However most agents have seen this message through previous iterations so we may not get accurate feedback unless fresh eyes see the message.
