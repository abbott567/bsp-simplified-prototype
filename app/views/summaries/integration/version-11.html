Research notes

When resuming a schedule agents were more confident with the imprisonment scenario. Agents understood that there may be some payments outstanding due to the anniversary date and the date that the claimant had been released from prison. The payments labelled as prevented were new but agents understood that these were the payments that they were not entitled to. When presenting agents with the resume scenario for rejected payments they were not primed as to what to do with the claim. They were told that someone had been asked to provide new bank details and they had done so. Agents went straight to change payment details and updated those confidently. Agents then needed prompting to check if this claimant was currently receiving any payments.
